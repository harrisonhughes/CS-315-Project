Truman State University

Truman State is a public university on the south side of Kirksville, Missouri. The school has gone through 6 name changes since it was first founded in 1867 by Joseph Baldwin, a trailblazer in education, and has held the name "Truman State", after Missouri native President Harry S. Truman, since 1996. The university has a 210 acre main campus housed between four streets, known as the quad, that contains 7 main academic buildings for 55 current undergraduate programs. The school boasts a stellar academic reputation, achieving consistently high marks from several reputable sources for both the strength of their programs, and the overall value of the school. From top-ranked health programs, to 3 conseutive bowl game wins for their football team, to a vibrant student population, Truman State is certainly a hidden gem just waiting to be discovered, right here in Kirksville, MO.
