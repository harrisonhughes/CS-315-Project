Normal School Era

From its' inception in 1867 to 1918, Truman State operated under the monicker of a "normal school", meaning the university was essentially a regional school to train teachers. Joseph Baldwin is the forefather of the school, and was a household name in the world of education prior to the opening of what was originally the North Missouri Normal and Commercial School. A year later, in 1968, the school dropped the "commercial" from the name to become North Missouri Normal School. Not much is known about these first few years. 2 years later, The Missouri General Assembly designated the school as the first public teachers college in Missouri, which came with another rename; the First District Normal School. This name stuck, and Joseph Baldwin's school would exist for another 48 years with this university name.
